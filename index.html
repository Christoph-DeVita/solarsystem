Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun and ranks as the third-largest planet in our solar system.

It has a diameter of approximately 31500 miles (50,700 kilometres).

Uranus's orbit around the Sun takes approximately 84 Earth years to complete. However, its rotation on its axis is relatively rapid, completing one rotation in about 17 hours.

Uranus's most striking feature is its axial tilt, which is nearly perpendicular to its orbital plane, causing it to appear to roll on its side as it orbits the Sun. This unusual orientation results in extreme seasons, with each pole experiencing 42 years of continuous sunlight followed by 42 years of darkness.

The surface of Uranus is composed primarily of hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane giving it its distinctive blue-green colouration.

The temperature of Uranus is estimated at 320¬∞F (-195¬∞C).

Saturn, known by many for its rings, is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest planet in our solar system.

The Planet has a diameter of approximately 72400 miles (ca. 116500 kilometres).

Saturn's orbit around the Sun takes approximately 29.5 Earth years to complete.

Saturn takes about 10.7 hours to rotate around its own axis, resulting in a noticeably oblate shape! The day length is similar, taking 10 hours and 34 min.

The atmosphere of Saturn is composed mostly of hydrogen and helium. Beneath lies a dense core of rock and ice, enveloped by metallic hydrogen.

The temperature of Saturn is estimated at around -216.4 ¬∞F (-138¬∞C).

Saturn has over 80 known natural satellites orbiting the planet. Titan, its largest moon, is of particular interest for Scientists. This is due to its dense atmosphere and methane lakes, making it a possible target for future exploration and potential colonisation.

Saturn is also surrounded by rings. Those reach thousands of kilometres into space. The rings are primarily composed of ice and rock particles.

Jupiter, reigns as the fifth planet from the Sun, is the largest planet in our solar system. With a diameter of approximately 86,881 miles (139,822 kilometres), Jupiter dwarfs all other planets, earning it the title of a gas giant.

Jupiter's orbit around the Sun takes around 11.86 Earth years to complete. Jupiter has a rapid rotation on its axis. A day on Jupiter lasts just under 10 hours, making it the fastest-spinning planet in our solar system.

The surface of Jupiter is composed of dense clouds of hydrogen and helium. This Cloud conceals Jupiter's solid core deep within.

The temperature on Jupiter is estimated at around -162¬∞F (-108¬∞C). Jupiter has an impressive number of 95 known satellites.

Jupiter's high mass exerts a gravitational pull. This pull is so powerful that it shapes the orbits of nearby celestial bodies significantly and even deflects potentially hazardous asteroids and comets away from Earth.

Jupiter has two red spots, those are due to massive impacts of other Systems, those being larger than Earth.

Earth, our beloved home planet, is the third planet from the Sun and the fifth-largest planet in our Solar System. It has a diameter of approximately 7900 miles (12700 kilometres).

Earth's orbit around the Sun takes approximately 365.25 days, defining the length of our calendar year. Back when the calendar was made, they counted as if the Earth would take 365 days to circle the Sun.

The Earth rotates on its axis, completing almost one rotation every 24 hours (23 hours m 56 minutes and 4 Seconds to be exact).

This provides us with the day and night cycle. That's what we call a day. If we didn't add one day every 4 years, we would fall one day behind. Which would also mean that the seasons would shift by one day each 4 years. This is impractical. That's why we simply add 1 day to our Calendar System every 4 years (the 29. of February) so we don't fall behind.

The surface of Earth is characterised by vast oceans and sprawling continents. Its atmosphere is primarily composed of nitrogen and oxygen.

The temperature ranges from -128¬∞F (-89¬∞C) to 125¬∞F (57¬∞C).

Due to a ‚Äúcoincidence‚Äù of many factors (rotation speed of the moon around earth and its own axis etc.), we only see one side of the moon from Earth.

Earth's hot core (the centre) is very important for us humans! The Earth's core is shaped like a ball. It contains primarily nickel and iron. The inner part of the Ball is solid due to immense pressure. The outer part is a hot liquid mostly containing nickel and iron (magma). The liquid moves a lot, thus the nickel and iron do. This creates electric currents, which generate a magnetic field.

This field is super important for us humans! For example, it protects life on Earth from harmful solar radiation (deadly for humans) and cosmic particles. Compasses use the magnetic field of the earth. That's useful for navigation on sea, land or even in the air.
